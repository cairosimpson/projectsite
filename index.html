Personal Project: Twitter Sentiment Analysis (2020)

After repeated arguments with my friends on the quality of newly-released music, I decided to combine two of my passions, music and data, to help settle these debates. Because I only learned C++ and Java in school, I decided to write this project in Python to teach myself the language.

The first step of the project was to write a program that scrapes Twitter comments to analyze. After collecting and storing the comments, I cleaned and normalized the data. I then created a custom ML model (sentiment-based Naive Bayes Classifier) to classify each comment as positive, negative, or neither.

Class Project: Houston Museum of Natural Sciences (2018)

At Rice I took an engineering design class to learn about the design process. I was placed in a group with three other students, and we were tasked to create a temperature and humidity regulator for their butterfly exhibit. Before, the museum would regulate humidity through wet towels and had no way to monitor the temperature. As a result, the butterflies had only a 70% emergence rate. Consequently, the goal of the project was to create an effective way of regulating humidity and temperature to increase the survival rate of the butterflies. We went through the iterative enginnering design process to create a final prototype.

As far as my role in the project, I played a key role in the desgin and implementation of the device. More importantly however, I faciliated group discussions and was deemed the leader in the post-project survey. We initially had accountability and cohesion issues, but I was able to ameliorate these problems quickly. To solve the accountability issue, I created a Google Doc where everyone would write exactly what they did so everyone could see if one member of the group was slacking. For the cohesion problem, one member of the group was rubbing the other members the wrong way due to his inflexibility. I talked to him one-on-one and kindly suggested that he should sometimes take a step back so everyone's input could be given proper consideration. My suggestion was well-received and consquently helped minimize conflict.
